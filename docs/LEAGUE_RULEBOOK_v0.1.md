# Minmini League Rulebook v0.1

Version: v0.1 (R&D Draft)
Last updated: 30 March 2026
Applies to: Season pilots and host-partner editions

## 1. Purpose

This rulebook defines the minimum standards for fair participation, project submission, validation, judging, scoring, and appeals in Minmini League.

## 2. Competition Model

- Minmini League runs as recurring editions under a season.
- Each edition may be hosted by an approved host partner.
- Minmini central governance owns standards, validation policy, scoring policy, and appeal process.

## 3. Eligibility

- Participants must meet edition registration requirements published by organizers.
- Team size must be between `2` and `8` members.
- Final team size limits for each edition are published in the edition notice.
- Solo participation is not eligible for League ranking.

## 4. Divisions and Tracks

- Divisions may be year-based or edition-specific, as announced before registration.
- Mixed-year teams may be placed in an Open division when defined by the edition.
- Teams must declare one project track at submission time.

## 5. Submission Requirements

Each submission must include:

- Project name and short description
- Team details and contributor mapping
- Public GitHub repository URL
- Demo video URL
- Optional deployment URL (required if edition says so)
- Basic README covering problem, solution, setup, and usage

Late or incomplete submissions may be marked ineligible based on edition rules.

## 6. Validation Framework

Validation is evaluated in three layers.

### 6.1 Eligibility Validation (Blockers)

Submission may be blocked from ranking if any blocker fails:

- Repo is inaccessible or invalid
- Missing mandatory deliverables
- Team-to-contributor mismatch beyond allowed threshold
- Severe plagiarism risk pending manual review
- Severe policy violation (security, integrity, conduct)

### 6.2 Common Quality Validation

Common quality checks include:

- Documentation quality
- Repo structure clarity
- Contribution timeline consistency
- Demo completeness

### 6.3 Track-Specific Evaluation

Projects are evaluated with track-aware criteria to ensure fairness across fields.

## 7. Judging and Scoring

- Judges score projects using published rubric.
- AI-assisted scoring may be used only where announced before event.
- Internal final score remains numeric for ranking, audits, and tie-breaks.
- Public result display may use grade bands (`S`, `A`, `B`, `C`, `D`, `E`).

## 8. Grade Bands (Default)

- `S`: 90-100
- `A`: 80-89
- `B`: 70-79
- `C`: 60-69
- `D`: 50-59
- `E`: below 50

Edition organizers may refine bands only before registration opens.

## 9. Tie-Break Rules

Tie-break order:

1. Higher internal final numeric score
2. Higher Innovation + Technical combined score
3. Higher consistency score across judges
4. Faster submission timestamp (if still tied)
5. Jury panel decision recorded with rationale

## 10. Code of Conduct and Fair Use

- All participants, mentors, judges, and organizers must follow code of conduct.
- Harassment, discrimination, deliberate sabotage, and academic misconduct are disqualifiable.
- AI tools are allowed only under edition policy and disclosure requirements.

## 11. Appeals Process

- Appeal window: within 24 hours after provisional results.
- Appeals must include evidence and specific claim.
- Appeal panel includes at least one central governance representative.
- Target decision SLA: 72 hours.
- Final decision and rationale are recorded for audit.

## 12. Result Publication Protocol

- Provisional internal ranking is locked before public reveal.
- Public reveal uses grade/rank format unless edition states otherwise.
- No heavy real-time recomputation during stage announcement.
- Any post-reveal correction must include published reason and change log.

## 13. Data and Consent

- Public identity fields (for example LinkedIn, alumni discovery) are opt-in.
- Users can modify consent preferences at any time.
- Personal data handling follows published privacy policy.

## 14. Rulebook Change Policy

- No breaking rule changes after registration opens.
- Clarifications may be published with timestamp and rationale.
- All hosts must use current approved version.

## 15. Minimum Edition Checklist

Before launch, edition must publish:

- Team size limits
- Tracks and judging rubric
- Validation requirements
- Grade display policy
- Appeal timeline
- Code of conduct and contact channel
